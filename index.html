An ISBN (International Standard Book Number) is a unique identifier for books. It's a 13-digit number that helps libraries, bookstores, and readers identify and order books. Every edition and format of a book needs its own ISBN.

While not legally required in all countries, an ISBN is highly recommended for professional book publishing. Most bookstores, libraries, and online retailers require ISBNs to list and sell books. It's essential for distribution and marketing.
